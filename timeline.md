# The Eternal Roman Empire Timeline
## *AUC Timeline with CE Equivalents*

---

# Part I — The Survival of Rome

## 786 AUC (33 CE) — The Death of Jesus
- Jesus of Nazareth is executed by Roman authorities in Judaea.
- Unlike in real history, the Roman government quickly minimizes the event and suppresses accounts of martyrdom.
- Christianity survives only as a small regional sect.
- Hellenistic paganism remains dominant across the Empire.

---

## 1041 AUC (288 CE) — Diocletian Rejects Partition
- Emperor Diocletian recognizes the Empire’s instability.
- Instead of dividing the Empire into Eastern and Western halves, he creates:
  - Regional administrative prefectures
  - A centralized imperial bureaucracy
  - A permanent professional civil service
- The Senate regains limited administrative influence.
- Rome remains politically unified.

---

## 1069 AUC (316 CE) — The Imperial Reform Accords
- Massive military reforms restructure the legions into:
  - Frontier armies
  - Rapid-response imperial field armies
- Merit-based officer advancement is introduced.
- Provincial corruption tribunals are established.

---

## 1120–1300 AUC (367–547 CE) — The Stabilization Era
- Germanic tribes are integrated into the Empire instead of replacing it.
- Large-scale infrastructure projects connect the Empire.
- Literacy expands across the provinces.
- The Imperial road system reaches its greatest extent.

---

# Part II — The Age of Discovery

## 1453 AUC (700 CE) — The Alexandrian Renaissance
- Massive scientific and philosophical revival begins in Alexandria.
- Steam mechanics, mathematics, astronomy, and engineering rapidly advance.
- Imperial academies spread across the Mediterranean.

---

## 1685 AUC (932 CE) — Oceanic Navigation Reforms
- Roman shipbuilders develop advanced deep-water vessels.
- Imperial exploration fleets begin Atlantic expeditions.

---

## 1762 AUC (1009 CE) — Discovery of the Western Continents
- Roman explorers land in North America.
- Initial settlements are established along the eastern coastline.

---

## 1810–1900 AUC (1057–1147 CE) — Expansion into the Americas
- Colonization accelerates.
- Native populations are gradually incorporated into the Empire.
- Massive roads, aqueducts, and ports are constructed.

---

# Part III — The Eternal Empire

## 2100 AUC (1347 CE) — The Pax Imperialis Doctrine
- The Empire formally adopts a doctrine of permanent internal peace.
- Provincial rebellions sharply decline.
- Imperial citizenship expands.

---

## 2300 AUC (1547 CE) — The Industrial Revolution of Rome
- Steam industry transforms the Empire.
- Massive industrial centers rise in:
  - Italia
  - Alexandria
  - Britannia
  - Nova Roma (North America)

---

## 2445 AUC (1692 CE) — The First Global Conflict
- Rival powers challenge Roman supremacy.
- Rome mobilizes industrial warfare on a global scale.
- The Imperial Navy becomes dominant worldwide.

---

## 2600–2700 AUC (1847–1947 CE) — The Modernization Era
- Electricity spreads across the Empire.
- Flight and radio communications revolutionize governance.
- The Senate evolves into a modern legislative institution.

---

## 2735 AUC (1982 CE) — The Space Initiative
- Rome establishes permanent orbital infrastructure.
- The Imperial Space Fleet is founded.

---

## 2778 AUC (2025 CE) — The Eternal Empire
### Current Emperor:
**Augustus Aelius Caesar**
- Ascends to the throne at age 16 following the death of his father:
  - Emperor Marcus Aelius Caesar

### Government Structure:
- Emperor serves as sovereign head of state.
- Senate functions as a modern legislative body.
- Senate Consul presides over the Senate.
- Governor’s General acts as imperial deputy confirmed by the Senate.

### Major Institutions:
- Tiberius Cordell Military Academy (TCMA)
- Imperial Senate Complex
- Imperial Space Fleet
- Provincial Governorship System

### Major Characters:
#### Augustus Aelius Caesar
- Young Emperor of Rome
- Blonde-haired ruler attempting to stabilize the Empire.

#### Pax Cassius
- Son of a slain American provincial governor.
- Rising revolutionary figure advocating self-rule and liberty.

#### Antonia Decima
- Elite military officer from humble origins.
- Graduate of the Tiberius Cordell Military Academy.

# Key Themes of the Setting
- Rome never falls.
- Hellenism remains the dominant religion.
- Ancient Roman traditions coexist with modern technology.
- The Empire possesses:
  - Television
  - Computers
  - Aircraft
  - Space travel
  - Advanced military technology
- Internal tensions grow between:
  - Imperial unity
  - Provincial autonomy
  - Republican ideals
  - Authoritarian stability

---

# Government Succession Process

## Upon the Death of an Emperor:
1. The designated heir immediately assumes provisional authority.
2. The Senate formally recognizes the succession.
3. The Governor’s General oversees governmental continuity.
4. The Imperial Coronation Ceremony occurs in Rome.
5. The new Emperor delivers their first address before the Senate.

---

# The State Religion

## Imperial Hellenism
- Greco-Roman paganism evolved into a modern organized state religion.
- Major gods remain culturally important:
  - Jupiter
  - Mars
  - Minerva
  - Apollo
- Religious tolerance exists across much of the Empire.
